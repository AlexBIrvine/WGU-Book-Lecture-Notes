2.18 - Fast Exponentiation

(Fast exponentiation uses successive squaring of values rather than repeated multiplication)

For a number bbb being raised to exponent xxx (so bxb^xbx), instead of multiplying b x times, instead follow these steps:

  1. Convert x into binary. (example: 575^757 becomes 5(111)25^{(111)_2}5(111)2​)
  2. For each "1" in that binary number, convert it into form 2x2^x2x, where x is the placement in the number. (example, 7= 222^222, 212^121, 202^020)
  3. Raise bbb to each of those exponents found in step 2. (Example: 522∗521∗5205^{2^2} * 5^{2^1} * 5^{2^0}522∗521∗520)

That makes for a faster method to find exponents for large numbers.
