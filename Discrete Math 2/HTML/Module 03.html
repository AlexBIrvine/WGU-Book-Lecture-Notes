1.13 - Asymptotic growth

Asymptotic complexity - Describes the behavior of the complexity function as n grows.

To find asymptotic complexity, remove all constants and focus in on the factor that grows the fastest.

Examples:

  1. f(n)=32n+50f(n) = 32n + 50f(n)=32n+50 has an asymtotic complexity of nnn, since we ignore 50 and 32.
  2. f(n)=64n2+32n+50f(n) = 64n^2 + 32n + 50f(n)=64n2+32n+50 has an asymtotic complexity of n2n^2n2, since we ignore constants and the slower growing nnn.
